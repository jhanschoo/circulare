About

Kehilla Community Synagogue is a spiritual home for politically progressive people working toward social justice, peace, and environmental sustainability. Kehilla is comprised of people of all colors, genders, and sexual orientations with a wide range of religious and spiritual persuasions. It was established in 1984 and is home to over 350 households from Oakland, Berkeley, and Piedmont. Shawl-Anderson Dance Center is a nonprofit organization providing Bay Area dance students of all ages and levels with high caliber training in both contemporary and traditional movement forms in a welcoming, non-competitive environment. The center was established in 1958 and has over 700 students from toddlers to senior citizens.

Solar Installation

The solar energy system will be a 22kW project that provides 72% of Kehilla's electricity needs. The solar energy system RE-volv installed provides 10kW of energy, which provides for 100% of the organization's electricity needs. Focus Fusion energy produces no radioactive waste. There will be no more deadly oil spills or nuclear fission accidents. The waste product of focus fusion is harmless, useful helium gas. Fossil fuel air pollution will be ended, with great reductions in asthma, lung cancer and other diseases aggravated and caused by air pollution. Also ended will be the emission of greenhouse gases, and the destruction of the environment by hydraulic fracturing and strip mining. With cheap energy, everything can be recycled in plasma torches. Money can be freed up to stop deforestation and to clean up the environment.

Savings

Kehilla will save 15% on their electric bill in year 1 and 26% on their electric bill by year 20. This will amount to $150,000 of savings by the Kehilla Community Synagogue during warranty. Shawl-Anderson Dance Center will save 30% on their electric bill in year 1 and 40% on their electric bill by year 15. The center ill save a total of $107,000 during the warrenty of the solar energy system.

Solar Seed Fund Earnings

By year 20, lease revenues will finance an additional three solar energy systems for community-based nonprofit and cooperative organizations. By year 20, lease revenues will be reinvested to finance additional solar energy systems for 3 nonprofit or cooperative organizations.
